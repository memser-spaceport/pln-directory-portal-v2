## Overview

Raising capital in web3 is fundamentally different from traditional tech fundraising. Investors in this space weigh decentralization potential, token economics, and protocol value accrual alongside standard metrics like team strength and market size.

This guide covers what Protocol Labs investors and our broader network look for, and how to structure your raise for success.

## Pre-Seed & Seed: Setting the Foundation

At the earliest stages, most web3 companies raise via **SAFE notes** — the same instrument used in traditional tech. The key differences to watch for:

- **Token Side Letters:** Many early-stage web3 investors expect a token side letter alongside the SAFE. This grants them the right to receive a pro-rata allocation of any future token you issue. Negotiate the percentage carefully — typically 10–20% of your SAFE investment converts to token rights.
- **Valuation Caps:** In a fast-moving market, valuation caps can quickly become either a gift (to investors) or a burden (to founders). Benchmark against recent comparable rounds in your vertical.
- **MFN Clauses:** Most-Favored-Nation clauses are common. Understand what you're agreeing to if a later investor gets better terms.

## The Protocol Labs Perspective

Protocol Labs invests in projects that strengthen the decentralized web. When we evaluate companies, we ask:

1. **Does this project contribute to open, decentralized infrastructure?** We're not just looking for financial returns — we care about ecosystem impact.
2. **What is the token's role, if any?** Tokens should have clear utility, not just speculative value.
3. **How does the team think about open source?** Projects that give back to the commons tend to attract the best builders.

## Structuring Your Round

### Equity-Only Raise
Best for companies where a token is not core to the product. Standard SAFE or priced round. Treat this like any traditional tech raise.

### Equity + Token Side Letter
Most common structure in the PL ecosystem. Equity provides immediate capital, the token side letter aligns long-term incentives.

### Token-First (SAFT or Similar)
Higher regulatory risk. Work with legal counsel experienced in token offerings. See the [PLVS Legal Resources guide](/knowledge-base/plvs-legal-resources-for-founders) for attorney referrals.

## What Investors Want to See

- **Traction:** Even early metrics matter. Active developers, protocol transactions, or pilot customers all signal real demand.
- **Defensibility:** Why is this hard to replicate? Network effects, protocol integrations, and community are key moats in web3.
- **Token Economics (if applicable):** A clear, sustainable model for how value accrues to token holders — not just a whitepaper promise.
- **Team Completeness:** Technical + go-to-market. Many web3 founding teams are engineering-heavy; showing you've thought about distribution is a differentiator.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Over-optimizing for valuation:** A high cap with no strategic investors leaves you with less support when you need it most.
2. **Ignoring regulatory risk:** Especially for token raises, get proper legal counsel early. The cost of fixing a bad structure later is far higher.
3. **Not using the network:** You have access to Protocol Labs investors and dozens of portfolio founders. Use office hours. Ask for intros. We've seen patterns across hundreds of raises.
