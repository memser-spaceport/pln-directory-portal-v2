## Overview

For international founders building in the US, navigating the immigration system is one of the most stressful and least-discussed challenges. The good news: web3 and deep-tech founders often have strong cases for prestigious visa categories — if you know how to structure them.

This guide covers the most common paths for Protocol Labs ecosystem founders and their key hires.

## The O-1A: Best Starting Point for Most Founders

The O-1A visa is for individuals with "extraordinary ability" in business, science, or technology. For technical founders and researchers with published work, open source contributions, or media coverage, this is often the fastest and most attainable path.

### O-1A Criteria (need to meet 3 of 8)
- **Prizes or awards** — hackathon wins, grants, fellowships
- **Membership in prestigious organizations** — Protocol Labs, Ethereum Foundation, etc.
- **Media coverage** — press about your work or company
- **Judging others** — reviewing grant applications, hackathon judging, academic peer review
- **Original contributions** — open source repos with significant adoption, published research
- **Authorship** — technical articles, whitepapers, conference talks
- **High salary** — relative to peers in your field
- **Critical role** — in a distinguished organization (like a PL portfolio company)

### Timeline
- Preparation: 4–8 weeks with a good attorney
- USCIS processing: 2–3 months standard; 15 business days with premium processing ($2,805)
- Valid for 3 years, extendable in 1-year increments

## EB-1A: Permanent Residency for Extraordinary Ability

The EB-1A is the green card equivalent of the O-1A. Same criteria, higher bar — but it's self-petitioned (no employer sponsorship needed), which is unique among employment-based green cards.

For founders who plan to stay in the US long-term, starting to build your EB-1A case early is worth the effort.

## The National Interest Waiver (NIW)

If your work has broad societal impact — decentralized infrastructure, open internet protocols, climate tech — you may qualify for an NIW EB-2 green card. This also doesn't require employer sponsorship.

PLCS can connect you with attorneys who specialize in building NIW petitions around web3 and protocol-layer technology.

## H-1B: What to Know

The H-1B lottery is a poor fit for most early-stage founders since it requires employer sponsorship and is subject to an annual lottery. However, it can be useful for key technical hires you're trying to bring to the US.

**H-1B for employees:**
- Annual cap: 85,000 visas; lottery in March each year
- Requires specialty occupation and bachelor's degree (or equivalent)
- 3-year initial period, extendable to 6 years

## For Your Team: L-1 Visas for Intracompany Transfers

If you have employees working for your company abroad and want to bring them to the US, the L-1 visa (L-1A for managers, L-1B for specialized knowledge) is often the cleanest path. Requires at least 1 year of employment abroad with the same company.

## Key Recommendations

1. **Start early.** Immigration cases take longer than founders expect. Begin your visa process 6+ months before you need US presence.
2. **Document everything.** GitHub contributions, press mentions, speaking engagements — every piece of evidence matters for O-1 and EB-1 petitions.
