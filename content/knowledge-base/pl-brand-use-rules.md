## Overview

As a PLVS portfolio company, you have a relationship with Protocol Labs that you can reference — but there are important guidelines around how to describe that relationship and use associated brand assets. Getting this wrong can create confusion in the market and occasionally legal issues.

This guide covers the key rules. When in doubt, reach out to your PLVS program lead before publishing.

## How to Describe Your Relationship to PL

### Correct usage
- "Backed by Protocol Labs Venture Studio"
- "A Protocol Labs Venture Studio portfolio company"
- "Built with support from the Protocol Labs ecosystem"
- "Supported by Protocol Labs"

### Incorrect usage
- ❌ "A Protocol Labs company" — implies you are an internal PL project, not a portfolio company
- ❌ "Part of Protocol Labs" — same issue
- ❌ "Protocol Labs' [Your Company]" — implies ownership/subsidiary
- ❌ Using "PL" or "Protocol Labs" in your company or product name without prior written approval

## Logo Usage

### PL Logo
- Do **not** use the Protocol Labs wordmark or logo without explicit written permission
- Do **not** modify the logo (recolor, stretch, add elements)
- You may use the logo in investor decks and press kits — contact your program lead for approved assets

### Co-branding
- Any co-branded materials (joint press releases, event collateral) must be approved by the PL comms team
- Allow 5–7 business days for review

## Describing Technology Built on PL Protocols

If your product is built on IPFS, Filecoin, libp2p, or other PL-originated protocols, you can and should reference this — it builds credibility. Correct framing:

- "Built on IPFS" ✅
- "Powered by Filecoin" ✅
- "Using libp2p for peer-to-peer networking" ✅
- "A Protocol Labs technology" ❌ (implies PL built your product)

## Press & Announcements

- If your announcement references Protocol Labs or PLVS, give the PL comms team 48 hours notice before publishing
- For major announcements (funding rounds, launches), coordinate with your program lead to align timing and messaging
- Do not quote PL spokespeople without their explicit approval

## When You Need Explicit Approval

- Using the PL or PLVS name/logo in advertising
- Claiming awards, certifications, or endorsements from PL
- Creating merchandise with PL branding
- Naming a product, feature, or initiative using "Protocol Labs" or "PL"
